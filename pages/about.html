THE VAGINA MONOLOGUES

Mission

The mission of the Vagina Monologues at UCSD is to raise awareness of sexual assault and domestic violence, one theatrical monologue at a time. All proceeds go to charity.

The Vagina Monologues at UCSD

The Vagina Monologues UCSD is an annual production promoting feminism and gender equality. Each winter quarter, 60+ women get together to put on a four-day show to talk about feminism, sexuality, and everything on our minds.

The Vagina Monologues UCSD has been around for 10 years and counting.

VDay

The Vagina Monologues were originally created by author and activist Eve Ensler in 1996. Eve interviewed 200 women about their vaginas; these responses created the show. Each monologue highlights different aspects of femininity, many of which are taboo in day-to-day life. This show represents women empowerment and creates a safe space to learn and think about what it means to be a woman.
