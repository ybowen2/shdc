THE VAGINA MONOLOGUES

What is "The Vagina Monologues"?

The Vagina Monologues is a series of interview made into a play by author, and activist Eve Ensler in 1996. Eve interviewed hundreds of women about their Vaginas, documenting the diverse experience they all have with their “down there”. Each monologue deals with different aspects of femininity; some were funny, some were sad.
The show is about raising awareness about the different spectrum of womanhood as well as creating a safe space to learn about their stories.

In UC San Diego, The Vagina Monologues has actually been around for over 10 years!
Each year, the show is directed by different UCSD students that are motivated to promote feminism (gender equality), thus each year the show differs depending on their focus.
This year, the directors Ariesela H-Smith, Alicia Powers and Armenuhi Avanesyan have emphasized the production on the intersectionality of feminism; Promoting gender equality through recognition of various degrees of oppression (i.e. race, gender, class, ability and ethnicity).

({Cause and Support})

The main goal of The Vagina Monologues is to use the empowering stories of the women before us to serve as a connection for further discussion and empowerment for the present community.
The efforts support victims of sexual assault and violence part of our proceeds from the show benefit the Center for Community Solutions, the only rape crisis center in all of San Diego County which dedicates itself to providing the resources needed for survivors to thrive. Last year we rose over $10,000 to donate to CCS.
This year, in an effort to extend the help within resources for victims, we will also give part of our proceeds to the License to Freedom Organization, whom promote non-violence and offer assistance to refugee and immigrant survivors in the Sand Diego region.

{(Event Info and Ticketing})

The Vagina Monologues is a seasonal play.
Each year the team starts recruiting women for the production around Week 3 of Fall quarter, and the shows are usually near Valentine’s Day during Winter quarter. Stay up to date by checking the Vag Mo google Calendar or the Facebook page.
